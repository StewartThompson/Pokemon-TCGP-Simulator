# Pokemon TCG Pocket — Official Battle Rules

---

## 1. Overview

**Pokemon TCG Pocket** is a streamlined, digital-first version of the Pokemon Trading Card Game designed for fast, competitive duels. Two players build 20-card decks, deploy Pokemon, attach energy, and battle until one player earns enough points from knockouts to win.

| | |
|-|-|
| **Players** | 2 |
| **Objective** | Earn 3 points by knocking out your opponent's Pokemon |
| **Skill Level** | Beginner–Intermediate |
| **Estimated Playtime** | 5–15 minutes per game |


---

## 2. Components

### Decks
Each player brings one 20-card deck. A deck must contain:
- Exactly **20 cards**
- At least **1 Basic Pokemon**
- No more than **2 copies** of any card with the same name

### Card Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Pokemon** | Placed on the field to battle. Come in Basic, Stage 1, and Stage 2. |
| **Item** | Trainer cards with immediate effects. Unlimited per turn. |
| **Supporter** | Powerful Trainer cards. Only 1 may be played per turn. |
| **Tool** | Trainer cards that attach to a Pokemon and provide a persistent bonus. Max 1 per Pokemon. |

### Pokemon Card Anatomy
Each Pokemon card has:
- **HP** — how much damage it can take before being knocked out
- **Type (Element)** — determines weakness interactions
- **Weakness** — takes +20 damage from the listed type
- **Retreat Cost** — number of energy discarded to retreat
- **Attacks** — up to 2, each with an energy cost, damage value, and optional effect
- **Ability** *(some cards)* — a special power, either passive or activated once per turn
- **Stage** — Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2

### Special Pokemon Variants
| Variant | KO Points | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Regular Pokemon | 1 | Standard |
| Pokemon-ex | 2 | Higher HP and stronger attacks |
| Mega Evolution ex | 3 | Instant win when KO'd; evolves normally from prior stage |

Mega Evolution ex cards follow normal evolution rules — they evolve from the specified pre-evolution (e.g., Mega Blaziken ex evolves from Combusken). They are not a separate mechanic; they are simply Stage 1 or Stage 2 Pokemon-ex with a 3-point KO value.

## 3. Setup

1. Each player shuffles their 20-card deck.
2. Each player draws **5 cards**. If a player has no Basic Pokemon in their opening hand, the hand is shuffled back and redrawn. This repeats until at least one Basic Pokemon is in hand.
3. Each player places one Basic Pokemon face-down as their **Active Pokemon**. Additional Basic Pokemon may optionally be placed face-down on the **Bench** (up to 3 bench slots).
4. A **coin flip** determines who goes first.
5. All cards are revealed and play begins.

> **Energy Zone:** Each player selects up to 3 energy types for their Energy Zone before the game. Each turn, one energy of a randomly chosen type from that selection becomes available to attach. If only 1 type is selected, that type is always generated.

---

## 4. Game Flow (Turn Structure)

Turns alternate between players. Each turn follows this sequence:

### Step 1 — Draw
Draw 1 card from your deck.
- There is **no hand size limit** — you may hold any number of cards.
- If your deck is **empty**, skip the draw. There is no penalty for running out of cards.
- **The first player skips the draw on turn 1** — they do not draw a card at all on their very first turn.
- When a card effect says "draw 2" but fewer cards remain, draw as many as are available — the effect does not fail.

### Step 2 — Energy Zone
One energy is generated from your Energy Zone and becomes available to attach this turn.

> **Exception:** The first player receives **no energy** from the Energy Zone on their very first turn.

### Step 3 — Main Phase
In any order, you may:

- **Play Basic Pokemon** onto an empty Bench slot
- **Evolve a Pokemon** (see Evolution rules) — you choose which Pokemon in play to evolve
- **Attach Energy** to any Pokemon in play — once per turn, you choose which Pokemon receives it
- **Play Item cards** — unlimited times
- **Play one Supporter card** — Supporters **can** be played on turn 1
- **Attach a Tool card** to a Pokemon that doesn't have one — you choose the target
- **Use a Pokemon Ability** — once per Pokemon per turn
- **Retreat** your Active Pokemon — once per turn
- **Attack** — ends your turn immediately *(not allowed on turn 1 for either player)*

> **Player choice:** The active player always chooses the target when playing cards, attaching energy, evolving, or activating effects — unless a card specifies otherwise.

### Step 4 — Pokemon Checkup (Between Turns)
After the active player's turn ends, status effects are resolved on their Active Pokemon (see Status Effects). Then the turn passes to the opponent.

---

## 5. Actions & Rules

### Playing Pokemon
- **Basic Pokemon** always go to the Bench when played from hand — they can never go directly to the Active slot, unless a specific card effect states otherwise.
- If a Bench slot is freed mid-turn (e.g., a Bench Pokemon is KO'd from spread damage), a new Basic can be played into that slot immediately — as long as the turn hasn't ended via an attack.
- **Stage 1 and Stage 2** Pokemon are played by evolving an existing Pokemon in play (see Evolution).

### Attaching Energy
- Once per turn, attach the energy from your Energy Zone to any Pokemon you have in play (Active or Bench).
- Energy stays on the Pokemon until it retreats, is knocked out, or an effect removes it.
- **The first player cannot attach energy on their very first turn** (the Energy Zone produces no energy that turn).

### Attacking
- Declare one of your Active Pokemon's attacks (up to 2 available per card).
- Pay the energy cost — the Pokemon must have the required energy attached.
- Attacking **ends your turn** immediately.
- **Energy is not consumed by attacking** unless the attack effect explicitly says so.
- **Neither player can attack on turn 1.** This applies to both the first and second player.

### Retreating
- Once per turn, swap your Active Pokemon with one from your Bench.
- **Discard energy** equal to the Active Pokemon's Retreat Cost from that Pokemon.
- A Pokemon with a Retreat Cost of **0 can always retreat** (no energy needs to be discarded), unless a specific card effect prevents it.
- When paying the retreat cost, **you choose which attached energy to discard**.
- The retreating Pokemon **keeps its current HP** and all remaining attached energy (minus what was spent on the Retreat Cost).
- Retreating **removes all status effects** from the retreating Pokemon.
- The Pokemon coming up from the Bench also **keeps its current HP and attached energy**.
- **Cannot retreat if Paralyzed or Asleep.**
- **Once per turn is a hard limit for voluntary retreats** — if you retreated earlier that turn and your Active is then KO'd from recoil, the promoted Pokemon cannot also retreat.
- If your Active is KO'd on your **opponent's turn**, the promoted Pokemon may retreat normally on your next turn (the once-per-turn flag resets each turn).
- If your Active Pokemon is knocked out and your Bench is **empty**, you **lose immediately** — even if you have a Basic Pokemon in hand. There is no opportunity to play it.

### Using Abilities
- Active abilities can be used **once per turn per Pokemon**.
- Passive abilities are always in effect and require no activation.

---

## 6. Evolution

- You **cannot evolve on turn 1** (neither player).
- A Pokemon **cannot evolve on the same turn it comes into play** — it must wait until your next turn.
- You **cannot evolve the same Pokemon twice** in one turn.
- You **cannot skip stages** (Basic → Stage 1 → Stage 2) — except when using **Rare Candy**, which allows skipping directly to Stage 2.
- Evolving **removes all status effects** from that Pokemon.
- **Tool cards remain attached** through evolution — they are not removed.
- Evolution applies to both Active and Benched Pokemon.

---

## 7. Status Effects

Status effects apply only to the **Active Pokemon**. They are resolved during the **Pokemon Checkup** phase between turns.

| Status | Effect During Checkup | How It's Cured |
|--------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Poisoned** | Pokemon takes **10 damage** | Card effect, evolve, or retreat |
| **Burned** | Pokemon takes **20 damage**, then flip a coin — **heads cures** Burn | Coin flip (heads), evolve, retreat, or card effect |
| **Paralyzed** | Pokemon **cannot attack or retreat**; automatically cured at the end of the **affected player's** next Checkup (they lose one full turn) | Cures automatically after 1 turn |
| **Asleep** | Pokemon **cannot attack or retreat**; flip a coin — **heads wakes up** | Coin flip (heads), evolve, or card effect |
| **Confused** | When the player attempts to attack, flip a coin — **tails causes the attack to fail** (no damage dealt, turn ends). **No self-damage.** | Evolve, retreat, or card effect |

### Stacking Rules
- Multiple status effects **can be active simultaneously**. For example, a Pokemon can be both Burned and Asleep at the same time — both effects apply.
- When multiple damage-dealing statuses are active (e.g., Poison + Burn), the **order of damage application does not matter** — both are applied during Checkup.
- Status effects can be applied to **your own Pokemon** by your own card effects (e.g., Snorlax puts itself to Sleep after attacking).

### Evolution
- Evolving a Pokemon **removes all its status effects**.
- All **attached energy carries over** to the evolved form.

### Abilities and Attacking
- You **may use an ability before retreating or attacking** on the same turn.
- Abilities on **Benched Pokemon** can be used directly from the Bench.
- **You cannot use an ability after attacking** — attacking ends your turn immediately.
- Each Pokemon may only use its ability **once per turn**. Evolving a Pokemon does not grant an additional ability use that turn.
- **Passive abilities are always active** and continue to work even when the Pokemon is Asleep, Paralyzed, or Confused — status conditions only block active attacks and retreat.
- An ability or Supporter that draws cards **cannot be activated** if it would bring your hand over 10 cards. This applies to abilities as well.

---

## 8. Weakness

- If the attacking Pokemon's type matches the defending Pokemon's **Weakness type**, deal **+20 bonus damage**.
- There is **no Resistance** mechanic in Pokemon TCG Pocket.

### Weakness Chart

| Defending Type | Weak To |
|----------------|---------|
| Grass | Fire |
| Fire | Water |
| Water | Lightning |
| Lightning | Fighting |
| Psychic | Darkness |
| Fighting | Psychic |
| Darkness | Fighting |
| Metal | Fire |

## 9. Scoring & Winning

### Earning Points
| Event | Points Awarded |
|-------|---------------|
| Knock out a regular Pokemon | **1 point** |
| Knock out a Pokemon-ex | **2 points** |
| Knock out a Mega Evolution ex | **3 points** (instant win) |

- Points are awarded to the player who dealt the knockout — even if the damage came from the defending player's own recoil effect or self-damage.
- Discarding a Pokemon via a card effect does **not** award points — only knockouts (HP reaching 0) do.
- **Bench Pokemon** that are reduced to 0 HP (e.g., from spread damage effects) are also knocked out and award points.
- The KO point value is determined by the **card currently in the slot** at the time of knockout. For example, if a Pikachu (1 point) evolves into Raichu-ex, it is worth 2 points at the time of KO.
- GX, V, and VMAX cards do not exist in Pokemon TCG Pocket. The only multi-point variants are Pokemon-ex and Mega Evolution ex.

### Winning the Game
The game ends immediately when any of the following occur:

1. A player reaches **3 points** → that player wins.
2. A player has **no Pokemon remaining** in play (Active and all Bench slots empty) → the opponent wins.
3. The game reaches the **turn limit** (~30 turns per player) without a winner → the game ends in a **draw**, regardless of either player's current point total.

---

## 10. Bench Promotion

When your Active Pokemon is knocked out:
- You **must immediately choose** a Bench Pokemon to promote to the Active slot.
- The promoted Pokemon **may retreat or evolve** on that same turn if eligible.
- If both players would reach 3 points simultaneously (e.g., both Actives knock each other out on the same attack), the game ends in a **tie**.
- If a spread damage effect knocks out **multiple Bench Pokemon** at once, points are awarded for **each** Pokemon knocked out.
- If a spread damage effect knocks out **multiple Bench Pokemon** at once, points are awarded for **each** Pokemon knocked out.

---

## 11. Card-Specific Rules

### Tools
- Each Pokemon may have at most **1 Tool** attached at a time.
- Multiple Tools can be attached in a single turn, each to a **different** Pokemon.
- Tool effects persist as long as the Pokemon is in play.
- Tools **remain attached when a Pokemon retreats** — they do not get discarded.
- Tools **remain attached when a Pokemon evolves** — they carry over to the evolved form.
- Tools can only be removed by specific card effects — they are otherwise permanent until the Pokemon is knocked out.
- When a Pokemon with a Tool is knocked out, **both the Pokemon and the Tool go to the discard pile**.

### Supporters
- Only **1 Supporter** may be played per turn.
- Supporters **can be played on turn 1**.
- A Supporter that draws cards cannot be played if it would require drawing more cards than are left in the deck — you must be able to draw the full amount.

### Items
- **Unlimited Items** may be played per turn.

### Supporters
- Only **1 Supporter** may be played per turn.
- A Supporter **cannot be played** if its draw effect would cause your hand to exceed 10 cards (e.g., a "draw 2" Supporter cannot be played with 9 cards in hand).

### Items
- **Unlimited Items** may be played per turn.

### Rare Candy
- Allows a Basic Pokemon to evolve directly into its Stage 2 form, **skipping Stage 1**.
- Evolution restrictions still apply (cannot use on turn 1, Pokemon must have been in play ≥1 turn).

### Deck Search
- When a card effect lets you search your deck for a card, you **shuffle your deck afterward**.

---

## 12. Penalties & Invalid Moves

- Illegal actions (insufficient energy, wrong target, etc.) are simply **not permitted** — the player must choose a valid action.
- Once an attack is declared and executed, it **cannot be undone**.
- There are no penalties for attempting an illegal move.

---

## 13. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Active Pokemon** | The Pokemon currently in the battling position |
| **Bench** | Up to 3 non-Active Pokemon slots behind the Active |
| **Energy Zone** | Generates 1 energy per turn from the player's selected types (up to 3) |
| **EX Pokemon** | A powerful Pokemon variant worth **2 points** when knocked out |
| **Evolve** | Replace a Pokemon with its higher Stage form |
| **KO (Knockout)** | When a Pokemon's HP reaches 0 |
| **Pokemon Checkup** | The between-turns phase where status effects are resolved |
| **Retreat** | Swap the Active Pokemon with a Bench Pokemon by discarding energy equal to the Retreat Cost |
| **Stage** | Evolution level: Basic → Stage 1 → Stage 2 |
| **Trainer Card** | Collective term for Item, Supporter, and Tool cards |
| **Weakness** | A type matchup that adds +20 damage when triggered |
